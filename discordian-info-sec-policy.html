The Five Pillars of Non-Bullshit Security

Our Information Security Policy isn't a compliance checkbox. It's the operational foundation of how we actually work. The Law of Fives manifests:

1. Confidentiality (Protect What Matters)

Not everything is secret. Not nothing is secret. Think. Customer data? Secret. Marketing plans? Internal. Open source code? Public. Classification based on actual impact, not paranoia.

Illumination: If everything is confidential, nothing is. Over-classification is security negligence disguised as diligence.

2. Integrity (Trust Your Data)

Data that lies to you is worse than no data. Version control. Audit logs. Hash verification. Digital signatures. Prove your data hasn't been tampered with‚Äîdon't just hope.

Illumination: Garbage in, garbage out‚Äîunless you also verify the pipe. Then you at least know it's garbage.

3. Availability (Uptime Isn't Optional)

Security that prevents access isn't security‚Äîit's a denial of service you're doing to yourself. Backups. Redundancy. Disaster recovery. Security enables business or it's just expensive failure.

Illumination: Perfect security with zero availability is indistinguishable from a successful attack.

4. Accountability (Know Who Did What)

Audit everything. Log everything. Trust no one (including yourself). When something breaks‚Äîand it will‚Äîyou need to know who, what, when, where, why. No shared accounts. No anonymous access to sensitive data.

Illumination: Accountability without logs is theater. Logs without review are data hoarding. Both are useless.

5. Non-Repudiation (Prove It Happened)

Digital signatures. Time stamps. Cryptographic proof. When someone claims "I didn't do that" or "that never happened," you need evidence, not arguments.

Illumination: In a world of deepfakes and gaslighting, cryptographic proof is the only truth left.

All hail Eris! The goddess of chaos teaches: security isn't about preventing all attacks‚Äîit's about knowing when you're under attack and responding appropriately.
