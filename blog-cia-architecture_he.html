Practical Wisdom for Architects

What did I learn building this system?

  1. Listen to the problem domain. If your architecture fights the natural structure of the problem, you'll fight maintenance forever. Riksdag data is hierarchical. Accept it. Don't force it into flat tables then wonder why queries are complex.
  2. Document synchronicities. When the same number keeps appearing, investigate why. Sometimes it's confirmation bias. Sometimes it's deep structure revealing itself. Learn to distinguish.
  3. Five is robust. Three components = fragile. Four = awkward. Five = resilient. Lose one of five and the system degrades gracefully. Lose one of three and it collapses. The pentagram is structurally sound.
  4. Balance over optimization. The best security with terrible UX serves no one. The fastest system that crashes daily disappoints users. The pentagram balances five concernsâ€”no concern dominates.
  5. Public architecture builds trust. Every diagram, every doc, every decisionâ€”public in our GitHub repo. Transparency isn't marketing. It's architectural reality. You can read our code, understand our choices, and judge for yourself.
