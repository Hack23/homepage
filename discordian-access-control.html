Why "Trust but Verify" Is Half Wrong

Think for yourself. The phrase "trust but verify" is security dogma. Here's the truth: Just verify.

Nothing is true. Everything is permitted. Except access without authentication, authorization, and audit logging.

Our Access Control Policy is public because access control through obscurity is like hiding keys under the doormat.

Illumination: Trust is a vulnerability. Verification is a control. Choose controls over vulnerabilities.

Zero Trust: Not Paranoia, Just Mathematics

"Zero trust" sounds paranoid. It's not. It's acknowledging reality:

1. Verify Every Request

No implicit trust. No "you're on the VPN so you're good." Every access request gets challenged. Always.

Illumination: The network perimeter dissolved in 2010. Anyone still trusting it is LARPing security.

2. Least Privilege Always

Minimum access required. Time-limited when possible. Revoke by default. Privilege creep is how admins become attackers.

Illumination: Admin access is like root access to reality. Use sudo, not stay logged in as god.

3. Assume Breach

Design like attackers are already inside. Because they probably are. Limit blast radius. Segment. Isolate. Monitor.

Illumination: If you can't detect malicious admin activity, you don't have security‚Äîyou have hope.

4. Log Everything

Who accessed what, when, from where. Audit trails or it didn't happen. Logs you don't review are data hoarding, not security.

Illumination: Logging without alerting is like having security cameras that nobody watches.

5. Encrypt in Transit

Network is hostile. Always. TLS everything. VPNs aren't trust‚Äîthey're encrypted untrust over untrusted networks.

Illumination: Cleartext on the wire is cleartext in the database after the breach. Plan accordingly.

The Five Factors of Not Getting Pwned

Multi-factor authentication isn't optional. Passwords alone are security theater. The Law of Fives demands:

  • Something you know (password) ‚Äî Easily stolen, phished, guessed
  • Something you have (hardware token) ‚Äî Better, but losable
  • Something you are (biometric) ‚Äî Can't change if compromised
  • Somewhere you are (geolocation) ‚Äî IP addresses lie
  • Something you do (behavior patterns) ‚Äî ML-based, fallible but useful

Use at least two factors. Three is better. One is negligence.
