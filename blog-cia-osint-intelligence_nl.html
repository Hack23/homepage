Common questions about OSINT, political intelligence gathering, and the 45 rules for watching the watchers.

What is OSINT and why is it important for political transparency?

OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) is the collection and analysis of publicly available information to create actionable intelligence. For political transparency, OSINT enables citizens to:

  • Monitor Government: Track activities and decisions in real-time
  • Analyze Patterns: Identify voting trends and legislative priorities
  • Ensure Accountability: Hold representatives responsible for their actions
  • Access Democracy: Use publicly available data to understand governance

What are the 45 rules for watching the watchers?

The 45 rules for watching the watchers are comprehensive OSINT methodologies for monitoring political activities, including:

  • Data Collection: Systematic gathering from official sources and databases
  • Pattern Recognition: Analyzing voting records and legislative behavior
  • Relationship Mapping: Tracking connections between politicians, policies, and parties
  • Temporal Analysis: Monitoring changes and trends over time
  • Transparency Verification: Ensuring accuracy and completeness of public records

How does CIA implement OSINT for Swedish parliamentary monitoring?

Citizen Intelligence Agency implements OSINT through automated data collection and analysis:

  • Data Sources: Swedish parliamentary APIs, riksdag.se, official government databases
  • Tracking: Voting records, committee assignments, legislative proposals, political affiliations
  • Analysis: Pattern recognition, accountability metrics, decision-making processes
  • Visualization: Network relationships, temporal trends, comparative analytics

What ethical considerations apply to political OSINT?

Political OSINT requires careful ethical balance between transparency and privacy:

  • Public Information Only: Collect only publicly available governmental data
  • Legal Compliance: Respect data protection laws (GDPR) and privacy regulations
  • Public vs Private: Focus on officials' governmental actions, not private lives
  • Accuracy: Ensure data correctness and provide proper context
  • Methodology: Document and disclose collection and analysis methods
  • Purpose: Aim for democratic accountability, not harassment
