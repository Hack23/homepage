The Five Steps of Not Getting Pwned By Known Vulns

1. Discover

You can't fix what you don't know exists. Asset inventory, vulnerability scanning, dependency checking. Know what you're running.

Shadow IT is just vulnerabilities you don't know about yet.

2. Assess

CVSS scores are guidance, not gospel. Consider exploitability, exposure, impact to your environment. Context matters.

CVSS 10.0 on an air-gapped system is less urgent than CVSS 7.0 on your internet-facing API.

3. Prioritize

You can't patch everything at once. Triage: Critical first, then high, then medium. Low when you have time. (You won't have time.)

Perfect is the enemy of patched. Patch critical first, optimize later.

4. Remediate

Patch, mitigate, or accept. Patch is best. Mitigation (WAF, network segmentation) is temporary. Acceptance better be documented.

Accepted risk is management's problem. Unmanaged risk is your problem at 3am.

5. Verify

Did the patch actually apply? Is the vuln actually gone? Rescan, validate, confirm. Trust, but verify. Mostly verify.

An unverified patch is Schr√∂dinger's security‚Äîboth applied and not applied until you check.

CVSS: Guidance, Not Gospel

Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) provides standardized severity ratings. It's useful. It's not perfect. It lacks your context:

CVSS Score Severity SLA Example
9.0-10.0 Critical Patch within 24-48 hours
7.0-8.9 High Patch within 1 week
4.0-6.9 Medium Patch within 1 month
0.1-3.9 Low Patch in next maintenance window

But context matters: Internet-facing? Actively exploited? Exploit code public? Data sensitivity? Adjust accordingly.

META-ILLUMINATION: CVSS tells you how bad the vuln is. You tell CVSS how bad it is for you.

Patch Process: Speed Balanced With Stability

Patching is balancing act‚Äîspeed vs stability:

  • Critical Systems ‚Äî Test quickly, deploy fast. Risk of patch < risk of exploit.
  • Production Systems ‚Äî Test in staging, deploy in maintenance windows. Minimize user impact.
  • Emergency Patches ‚Äî For actively exploited zero-days. Deploy fast, roll back if needed.
  • Rollback Plan ‚Äî Always have one. Patches break things. Be ready to revert.

Operation Mindfuck the attackers: Patch faster than they can weaponize.
